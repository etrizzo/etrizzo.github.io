About Me

As a programmer, I believe that my role is to empower my teammates to do their work as quickly and effectively as possible. Whether working on gameplay features or on developer tools, I pride myself on my ability to identify the core of the problem with my team members and translate that into an efficient, intuitive solution designed for fast iteration through data-driven designs and scalable interfaces. I firmly believe that the best games are made through strong communication, and a healthy, collaborative studio culture - and I strive to support that kind of environment through my work.

When working on solo projects, I like to make small, standalone sandboxes to explore problems that are interesting to me. I love game jams, and often start my own project for online jams like Ludum Dare, but I often decide to spend the weekend diving deep on an interesting technical problem instead of putting together a "finished" game. In particular, I enjoy playing with procedural content generation, visual effects, and generative art. I often work in my own engine using C++/OpenGL, or in small, creative game engines such as Pico8.

Outside of work, I like to play a wide variety of games. Beyond any specific genre, I enjoy games that find ways to feel new and exciting. Some of my favorite games from the past few years are Outer Wilds, Subnautica, Tears of the Kingdom, Stardew Valley, Rimworld, Unpacking, and Celeste. Outside of gaming, I like to spend my free time gardening, making pottery, and hiking around Seattle.
